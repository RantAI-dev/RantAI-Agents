# HorizonLife Policy & Underwriting Guide

## Overview

This document provides detailed policy terms, coverage limits, exclusions, and underwriting guidelines for all HorizonLife insurance products. This is an internal reference for agents and underwriters.

---

## Life Insurance Underwriting

### Risk Classification

| Class | Description | Premium Modifier |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Preferred Plus | Excellent health, no tobacco, ideal BMI, no family history | 0.70x (30% discount) |
| Preferred | Very good health, no tobacco, minor health issues acceptable | 0.85x (15% discount) |
| Standard Plus | Good health, minor conditions controlled with medication | 0.95x (5% discount) |
| Standard | Average health, meets basic requirements | 1.00x (base rate) |
| Substandard (Table 1-8) | Health issues requiring rated premium | 1.25x - 2.50x |
| Decline | Uninsurable conditions | N/A |

### Medical Exam Requirements

| Coverage Amount | Ages 18-40 | Ages 41-50 | Ages 51-60 | Ages 61+ |
|-----------------|-----------|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Up to $250,000 | No exam | No exam | Paramedical | Full medical |
| $250,001 - $500,000 | No exam | Paramedical | Full medical | Full medical |
| $500,001 - $1,000,000 | Paramedical | Full medical | Full medical | Full medical + EKG |
| Over $1,000,000 | Full medical | Full medical + EKG | Full medical + EKG + stress test | Case-by-case |

### Tobacco Use Policy

- **Smoker rates apply if**: Any tobacco product used in the last 12 months (cigarettes, cigars, chewing tobacco, e-cigarettes, nicotine patches/gum)
- **Reclassification**: Can apply for non-smoker rates after 12 months tobacco-free with cotinine test
- **Cigar exception**: Occasional cigar use (≤12 per year) may qualify for non-smoker rates at underwriter discretion

### Occupation Categories

| Category | Risk Level | Examples | Premium Impact |
|----------|-----------|---------|---------------|
| Class 1 | Low | Office workers, teachers, IT professionals | Standard rates |
| Class 2 | Moderate | Nurses, electricians, retail managers | +10-15% |
| Class 3 | High | Construction workers, long-haul truckers, firefighters | +25-50% |
| Class 4 | Very High | Mining, logging, offshore oil workers | +75-150% or decline |
| Hazardous | Extreme | Stunt performers, professional race car drivers | Individual assessment |

### Common Exclusions (All Life Products)

- Suicide within first 2 years of policy (contestability period)
- Death during commission of a felony
- Material misrepresentation on application
- War or act of terrorism (unless War Risk rider purchased)
- Death while under the influence of non-prescribed controlled substances

## Health Insurance Underwriting

### Coverage Tiers

| Tier | Monthly Premium Range | Deductible | Out-of-Pocket Max | Co-pay (PCP/Specialist) |
|------|--------------------|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Bronze | $200-$350 | $6,000 | $8,500 | $40 / $80 |
| Silver | $350-$500 | $3,000 | $6,500 | $30 / $60 |
| Gold | $500-$700 | $1,000 | $4,500 | $20 / $40 |
| Platinum | $700-$1,000 | $250 | $2,500 | $10 / $20 |

### Pre-Existing Conditions Policy

- **Guaranteed issue**: All applicants accepted regardless of pre-existing conditions during open enrollment
- **Special enrollment**: Qualifying life events (marriage, birth, job loss) trigger 60-day special enrollment
- **Waiting periods**: None for pre-existing conditions
- **Chronic condition management**: All tiers include chronic disease management programs at no additional cost

### Network Types

| Network | Provider Choice | Out-of-Network Coverage | Referral Required |
|---------|----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| HMO | Must use in-network except emergencies | No (except emergencies) | Yes |
| PPO | In or out of network | Yes, at higher cost (40/60 split) | No |
| EPO | Must use in-network except emergencies | No (except emergencies) | No |
| POS | In or out of network | Yes, at higher cost | Yes for specialists |

### Common Exclusions (All Health Products)

- Cosmetic surgery (unless medically necessary, e.g., post-mastectomy reconstruction)
- Experimental or investigational treatments not FDA-approved
- Long-term custodial care (nursing home)
- Services received outside the US (unless Emergency Travel rider purchased)
- Work-related injuries covered by Workers' Compensation
- Dental and vision (unless added as supplemental riders)

## Home Insurance Underwriting

### Coverage Limits and Structures

| Coverage Type | What It Covers | Typical Limit |
|--------------|---------------|--------------|
| Dwelling (Coverage A) | Home structure | Replacement cost of home |
| Other Structures (Coverage B) | Detached garage, fence, shed | 10% of Coverage A |
| Personal Property (Coverage C) | Furniture, electronics, clothing | 50-75% of Coverage A |
| Loss of Use (Coverage D) | Living expenses if displaced | 20% of Coverage A |
| Personal Liability (Coverage E) | Lawsuits from injuries on property | $100,000 - $500,000 |
| Medical Payments (Coverage F) | Guest injuries, no-fault | $1,000 - $5,000 per person |

### Deductible Options

| Deductible | Premium Impact | Best For |
|-----------|---------------|---------|
| $500 | Highest premium | Frequent small claims expected |
| $1,000 | Standard premium | Most homeowners (recommended) |
| $2,500 | 10-15% discount | Risk-tolerant homeowners |
| $5,000 | 20-25% discount | High-value homes, financially secure |
| Percentage (1-5% of Coverage A) | Varies | Hurricane/wind/hail zones |

### Risk Factors Assessed

| Factor | Impact | Notes |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Roof Age | High | Roofs over 20 years may require inspection |
| Distance to Fire Station | Medium | Over 5 miles = higher premium |
| Claims History | High | 2+ claims in 5 years = surcharge or decline |
| Credit Score | Medium | Used in most states for rating |
| Dog Breeds | Medium | Certain breeds excluded or surcharged |
| Swimming Pool | Medium | Requires fencing and liability coverage |
| Trampoline | Low-Medium | May require signed liability waiver |
| Home-based Business | Low | May need separate endorsement |

### Common Exclusions (All Home Products)

- Flood damage (requires separate NFIP or private flood policy)
- Earthquake damage (available as endorsement in applicable areas)
- Normal wear and tear, maintenance, or gradual deterioration
- Pest/insect/rodent damage
- Government action (eminent domain, zoning)
- Nuclear hazard
- Intentional loss or damage by insured
- Mold (unless result of a covered peril, limited to $10,000)

## Underwriting Decision Guidelines

### Auto-Approve Criteria (Fast Track)

- Life: Ages 18-45, coverage ≤$250K, no tobacco, no major health conditions, BMI 18-30
- Health: Ages 18-64, no current hospitalization, employer-sponsored or open enrollment
- Home: Built after 1990, no claims in 5 years, credit score 700+, roof age <15 years

### Refer to Senior Underwriter

- Life: Coverage >$1M, ages >60, substandard classification, foreign travel to high-risk areas
- Health: Chronic conditions requiring specialty treatment, prior coverage gaps >63 days
- Home: Claims history of 2+ in 3 years, high-value homes (>$1M), unusual construction, coastal/flood zones

### Decline Guidelines

- Life: Active terminal diagnosis, incarceration, currently hospitalized, age >80 for new policies
- Health: Currently hospitalized (defer to after discharge), fraudulent application history
- Home: Property condemned or in active foreclosure, prior arson investigation, 3+ claims in 2 years

## Compliance Notes

- All policies must include state-mandated disclosures
- Free-look period: 30 days for life, 10 days for health, varies by state for home
- Cancellation: Pro-rata refund for policyholder-initiated cancellation
- Non-renewal: 60-day written notice required
- Privacy: HIPAA compliance for all health information, GLBA for financial data
- Anti-discrimination: Rating must comply with state insurance regulations, no discrimination based on protected classes
